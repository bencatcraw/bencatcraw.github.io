31 Aug 2023

Current Interests

Currently, my main focus in ICS is working on game development. I grew up playing a lot of video games after my brother introduced me to Minecraft when it first released. Ever since then I had been hooked. I spent most of my younger years playing games but once I turned around 15 my focus turned towards development. I had always been interested in making my own games and my brother had shown me unity for the first time after he took a class on it in university.

I immediately started doing everything I could to learn game development and have been experimenting with ideas for around five years. One of my biggest shortcomings however, is that I always struggle to be proud of what I have made, which usually results in me scrapping the project and moving to something new. Now, I’m happy to say being in university has given me the opportunity to work collaboratively with others interested in game development and I already have multiple complete projects with more to come.

Software Engineering

Going into this class, I have very little software engineering experience. Other than the classes I’ve taken in the ICS track like 111, 211, 212, and 369, I only really know the programming side of things. I also have very little experience with Javascript or HTML but so far it has not been too hard to pick up.

Im excited to learn more from this class and I already have practical ideas of software I could make. For example, I spend a lot of my time helping out with the technical side in the gaming lab on campus. Currently, for students wanting to play games between classes, they can show up to the lab, sign in through a google form, and then ask to play at a PC. Right now this process is all manual, the google form just goes into a spreadsheet, and in a seperate spreadsheet they keep track of who is playing on what computer and for how long. After taking this class, I would like to be able to create a program that could automate this process for them which would improve the wait times for people hoping to play games before their next class.

I am also currently in the VRAR class with Jason Leigh, and a few of our projects in that class include creating augmented reality applications for mobile devices, so hopefully I will be able to combine my skills from both classes and create an interesting and practical phone app that people could actually use.

The Future

In the future, after I get my degree, I plan to apply for a role in either game development or software engineering as they both interest me and often share some similar design principles.
