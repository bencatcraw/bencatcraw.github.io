AI's impact on my life

20 Nov 2023

Introduction

Artificial intelligence, or “AI”, is a new and incredibly powerful version of software that allows computers to recognize patterns and create things in a similar way humans can. While AI isn’t quite to the level of intelligence that a human can produce, it’s improving every day and many fear it could take our jobs in the future. There are thousands of companies out there now that offer AI, but I have only used a few of the most commonly known ones such as ChatGPT and GitHub Co-Pilot.

Personal Experience with AI

Experience WODs e.g. E18

For these kinds of WODs where I had more time to work with, I chose not to use AI to challenge myself.

In-class Practice WODs

Similar to the Experience WODs, I tried to challenge myself with the practice WODs, being that they aren’t graded and chose not to use AI unless I was completely lost.

In-class WODs

For most of the In-Class WODs, especially the more lengthy ones, I would attempt them first with my own knowledge and if I couldn’t figure it out or fix a bug I would turn to ChatGPT.

Essays

All essays I chose to write myself, I found in the past generating essays with ChatGPT often gives very basic and boring results and prefer to write them myself.

Final project

So far, I haven’t used any AI on the Final Project, but once we get into the more difficult stuff within the application, I will definitely be utilizing ChatGPT.

Learning a concept / tutorial

I usually prefer looking up a tutorial video or forum before going to AI for new concepts.

Answering a question in class or in Discord

I don’t think this is a very useful application for AI because it doesn’t understand what our class is.

Asking or answering a smart-question

I rarely ever utilize AI just to ask a question and prefer a search engine like Google.

Coding example e.g. “give an example of using Underscore .pluck”

I have tested coding examples on multiple occasions and ChatGPT seems to always give a reasonable answer providing your input makes sense.

Explaining code

Usually when I’m given code to work on that I don’t understand sometimes its nice to ask AI about how it works befored diving in.

Writing code

I have had a lot of success with AI writing code, especially Co-Pilot, which almost always knows what I want to do in IntelliJ

Documenting code

Sometimes when I’ve tried learning more about what the code I wrote does, I ask ChatGPT to read and add comments to my code and it is often very helpful.

Quality assurance e.g. “What’s wrong with this code?” or “Fix the ESLint errors in …”

This is probably the most common use of AI for me. I often use AI to fix errors or answer questions on why my code isn’t working. The answers are often helpful and in-depth.

Impact on Learning and Understanding

I think AI has had a positive impact on my learning and understanding in this class because it almost acts as an alternate instructor in some cases such as asking about how a certain piece of code works.

Practical Applications

Outside of our class AI has lots of applications. For example, when I was working on the HACC, I used ChatGPT to take a large list my teammate compiled of healthcare center information and requested it organize it into a meteor database json file. I also use it often when developing games in my free time, as it is very good at explaining game design concepts.

Challenges and Opportunities

I’ve ran into little challenges using AI. Sometimes it’s not answering exactly what you want so you need to be more descriptive. In the future, I think AI will improve even more and be more accurate in its requests in software engineering.

Comparative Analysis

Even though AI is very powerful and helpful in certain circumstances, I think it can be detrimental if not regulated to a certain degree in an educational setting.

Future Considerations

Because of how much an impact AI already has on software engineering, I think its important all aspiring software engineers learn how to utilize AI as it will probably become a requirement in the future.

Conclusion

Overall, I think AI is really useful in most circumstances and should be utilized when possible to improve consistency and effeciency. And if AI does take my job in the future, I’ll go back to working on a fish farm.
